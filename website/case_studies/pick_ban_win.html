PickBanWin

PickBanWin is an AI-driven tool designed to analyze enemy drafts and recommend winning team compositions. It leverages historical data and recent discussions to deliver actionable insights during the drafting phase. It helps the the user understand, given the hero pool and their team's playstyle and capabilities, what are the best heroes to pick and ban in order to maximize the chances of winning the game.

Overview

In the world of Multiplayer Online Battle Arena (MOBA) video games such as Dota2 and League of Legends where each player controls one singular character known as the "hero" or "champion" [will be referred to as hero for the rest of this case study], the draft phase is crucial for team success in competitive play. Each team takes turns picking a hero for their team to play and banning a hero to be removed from the hero pool entirely, preventing either side of picking that hero. There is a limited amount of time to choose which hero to pick and ban and with the game constantly changing either from game patches or swings in the meta game, a successful hero picking and banning stage may be the key to priming the team to a win or leading them to certain defeat.

Goal

We are building a 0-1 here so the main goal is to find product-market fit.

Constraints

Due to technical reasons, we cannot hook into a currently running game and gather live information.

Customer segmentations

There are several types of players that we could possibly target:
  • Ultra competitive players that have this tool running as they are playing the game. These people have a high degree of understanding in the game and keep up with the current discussion as the game updates. However, they may not know the exact precise win/loss statistics as they shift.
  • Players that want to theorize possible combinations. These are people that visit the tool but are not under a time crunch of an actual game. They can play around with the draft by themselves or with anothe person simulating the other team.
  • Players that do not know the game well and want suggestions to be more like decision makers. These are people that are trying to not be overwhelmed and will trust this suggestion from the tool without reservation.
Based on our goal of finding a product-market fit, the greatest value we can get is from the group of ultra competitive players that want to use this as they play. They will be very inclined to use this tool if it thinks it can help them and since they want to win, they will have higher chance of providing feedback to improve the tool than the other two groups which are more casual.

Use Cases and Pain Points

  • The Hero Suggester
    Given the current state of the draft, they need to understand what the next hero to pick or ban should be that would maximize their chances of winning.
    • Pain Point
      With a typical MOBA game having over 100 heroes to choose from and therefore seemingly endless combinations along with other factors such as your both teams' strengths and weaknesses, it may be overwhelming to decide which heroes to pick and ban. There are simply too many factors that comes into play and factors such as the opinion of the other team members and the clocking counting down only adds to the stress. This leads to:
      • Paralysis Analysis
      • Influenced by several teammates leading to a non-cohesive strategy
      • Missing obvious picks or bans or making mistakes
  • The Player Scouter
    Scout out the their teammates and opponents and understand what heroes they usually play and how well they do with each hero.
    • Pain Point
      Besides knowing good hero synergy compositions to pick and ban, it is important to understand what heroes your teammates and opponents like to play when formulating your plan. Currently. this requires loading up each player's profile and looking at their hero statistics individually which is a time consuming process and difficult to organize with many browser windows open.
  • The Hero Pairing Suggester
    Understand what their gameplan is and how their hero pairings (which heroes to play together) work.
    • Pain Point
      Certain heroes work better together than others and this differs at different stages of the game. It is crucial to have this data on hand when selecting not just hero picks and bans, but also how they will be played on in the game. Currently, this is done by instinct and experience which may be biased or outdated -- both of which will not be aligning with the data.

Prioritized Solutions

Given the use cases and pain points, the solutions in a priority order are:
  • The Hero Suggester
    At both the beginning of the draft (if the user drafts first) and after every hero pick/ban choice is made, PickWinBan will suggest three heroes for the user to select from based on a variety of factors such as already picked and banned heros, statistics, patch notes, and list of players and opponents. With each hero suggestion, PickWinBan will provide a reason as to why this hero was suggested and what kind of role or playstyle this hero needs to play in order to maximize the chances of winning. Lastly, if it is a hero pick suggestion, it will suggest a player on the team that is most suitable to play.

    The reason this is first is because this is what we are betting on as the core functionality of the tool that will attract users. Other features, while they can be standalone, are expected to fold into this decision making process here.

  • The Player Scouter
    Provide a way to quickly see what the top three heroes their teammate and opponents like to play and how well they do with each hero.

    The reason this is second is because it allows people to feel like they are being heard and respected. Even if a hero is good for the team overall, it may not be good or fun for a specific player which would lead to lower morale.

  • The Hero Pairing Suggester
    After the user has confirmed all their and their opponent's hero picks, PickWinBan will predict how the opponent will play their hero pairings (or groupings if more than two heroes together or it could be solo). PickWinBan will then suggest to the player how to play out their hero picks to both counter the opponent's predicted move and also to make sure the two heros are as synergistic as possible.

    The reason this is third is because this is functionality is pretty easily gathered from already existing websites. It is provided here to "complete" the suite of tools and make this a one-stop-shop but it is not the core functionality of the tool.

Dependencies

  • Data Retrieval
    The biggest dependency is on the game developer's APIs to be able to retrieve match information but also to provide the data necessary to make the suggestions. This includes hero statistics and player statistics. Using Dota2 and League of Legends as an example, this is reliant on APIs such as Valve's Web API and Riot Games API to provide the data we need to make the suggestions.
  • Patch Notes
    We will be dependent on the game developers to release patch notes and their detailed information, especially at the beginning of a patch cycle. This information is used for us to make suggestions before enough matches have been played where we can have more reliability on the data.

Risks and Mitigation

  • Risk of game not reaching popularity
    If the game does not launch well and the player count is small or the game does not develop competitively, then this feature will not be used. One way to mitigate this is to bet not just on this game but rather this genre of games. This allows us to hedge our bets and as long as we create this tool generically enough, it should be easy enough to port over. Best scenario is that if multiple games in this genre are popular, then we can have a tool that owrks for all of them and we can have a larger user base.
  • Risk of not getting enough data to provide accurate and useful suggestions
    This will definitely occur anytime there is a patch with either new content or game balance changes. This is where GenAI can shine. We can provide weighting for suggestions based on statistics or reasoning of the patch notes. We can ask the LLM to provide us for these weights. At the beginning of the patch cycle, the LLM can decide whether this patch is similar or different to the previous patch. If it is similar, we can give more weights using the previous patch's data. If it is different, we can increase the weight that the LLM suggests based on the patch notes. In both cases, as we progress more and more into the patch cycle, we can increase the weights of the suggestions based on the data.

Metrics for Success

  • Daily Active Users
    This will help us know if we even have an audience or not. We should also put, as a background, the daily active users of the game in general.
  • 50th percentile usage sessions per 4 hours
    This is a frequency measure and will let us know whether users continue using us over the course of one play session. Games, along with queue times, can often be more than an hour long so we will set somewhat of an arbitrary 4 hour window to measure how long a session somebody plays the game for. We can measure this with an anchor time of hour H and the window stretching back to hour H-4.
  • Week over Week Retention rate
    This will let us know if users, after they have had time to digest their results and how useful the tool is, comes back for more.
  • 50th percentile percentage of times the user followed the suggestion given
    Suppose in each drafting round, the user has an opportunity to pick 6 and ban 3 heroes. This means we have 9 opportunities to provide suggestions. If the user followed our suggestion 6 times, that would be 66.67%.
  • Percentage of matches won if the user followed our suggestion more than 75% of the time
    While it is expected that the user can still lose even if they followed our suggestion 100% because of skill issues, we should at least be above 50% in this regard to even be viable. If we are below 50%, then we should look into the suggestions and see if they are actually good or not. We also expect the user to not follow us 100% of the time but still be able to get good results.

Future Roadmap

  • Play Style
    The user can ask PickWinBan to suggest heroes to pick and ban to suit their play style (pick the heroes that suit and ban the heroes that would be a good counter to their play style). For example, a team may wish to be really strong early game and are fine fading away in power as the game goes on. PickWinBan will take that into account and suggest picks and bans accordingly to fit their play style.
  • Build suggestion
    In addition to suggesting hero picks and bans, given both team's compositions, PickWinBan can also suggest which skills to select and level up and also items to buy. This ensures that both the strategic and tactical choices of the game are unified as one clear vision and really lets the user focus on the game rather than following a random guide on the internet that may not fit this strategy.
  • Restricted Hero Picking set
    The user may only choose to play with a certain subset of heroes for a number of reasons (fun, familiarity, want to try new heroes). The user can ask PickWinBan to suggest the best hero pick choice amongst this restricted set of heroes. This will allow the user to have a more personalized experience and not feel like they are being forced to play heroes that they do not want to play.
  • Disregard and re-suggest PickWinBan may suggest a hero to pick or ban based on a specific reason. If the user wishes to dismiss this reason, they can and PickWinBan will retry the hero suggestion process for picking and banning.
